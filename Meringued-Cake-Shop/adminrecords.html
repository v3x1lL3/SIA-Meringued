Records

Track audits, expenses, receipts, and purchases—each with a date—so you can review and report on your shop’s activity.

What is each record type for?

EOD inventory — One row per ingredient with quantity on hand per day (today updates when you open Records). On the EOD inventory tab, use the date range under the tabs + Apply to view past days. Use Inventory audit for each stock in/out line.

Inventory audit (with date) — Each stock in/out movement with a date (different from EOD inventory, which is totals on hand per day).

Sales receipts (with date) — Record of sales or proof of sale (e.g. “Receipt #001 – 2 cakes”, amount in ₱).

Purchase expenses (with date) — Auto-created when you Stock In on Inventory → Ingredients or Miscellaneous (qty × Price ₱/unit when set). References: Inventory stock-in (ingredients) and Inventory stock-in (miscellaneous) (supplies). Miscellaneous is logged even if unit price is still ₱0 so every restock appears; set price on the row (Done) to track spend. Use Add record for other purchases (supplier, invoice, etc.).
